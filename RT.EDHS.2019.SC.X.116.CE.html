The science of optics is the application of lenses and mirrors for practical purposes.

First application of lenses was making spectacles.

  • Eye has a lens, the cornea, which focuses light on the retina.

  • If light does not focus properly, images seen are blurry.

  • Corrective lenses refract light so that it hits the retina again.

    • Some people cannot focus on objects far away.

    • Some need glasses for reading.

    • Bifocals are used for people who need help with both.

Spectacle technology was adapted to make the first microscopes.

  • Compound microscope uses two lenses for magnification.

    • Objective lens is the lens nearer to the specimen being studied.

    • Eyepiece is the lens closer to the viewer.

    • Lenses are typically convex lenses.

  • Simple microscope has only one convex lens, like a magnifying glass.

  • Most compound microscopes have more than one objective lens, with different magnifications, that can be rotated into position.

  • Objectives are typically higher in magnification than the eyepiece.

  • Total magnification for a microscope is found by multiplying the magnification of the eyepiece and the objective lens.

At the same time microscopes were developed, refracting telescopes were invented.

  • Refracting telescopes have two lenses at either end of a long tube.

    • Objective lens is a convex lens and at the far end of the tube.

    • Eyepiece is a concave lens where the viewer looks.

  • Magnification is determined by the length of the tube and the diameter of the lenses.

  • The objective lens focusses light to the eyepiece where it is refracted again to the eye.

  • Size and cost of the lenses and telescope limit the ability to view objects in the Solar system.

  • Suffer from color distortion around the edges called chromatic aberration.

Reflecting telescopes were developed about 75 years after refracting telescopes.

  • Combines mirrors and lenses

  • Light enters and reflects from concave mirrors and travels to a flat mirror at an angle that directs the light to the eyepiece.

  • Can produce better magnification with smaller and less expensive telescopes

  • Does not suffer from chromatic aberration

  • Hubble space telescope is a reflecting telescope.

Catadioptric telescopes were developed the late 1800s.

  • Integrates lenses into the design more than common reflecting telescopes

  • More magnifying power than a refracting telescope, but not as much as reflecting telescopes

  • More compact than the typical reflecting telescope

Cameras use lenses to focus light on a film or digital sensor.

  • Cameras often have more than one lens.

  • When focusing a camera, operator moves the lenses so that they focus the light at the correct point.

  • Film can be converted to a picture with chemicals.

  • Digital cameras save the image as a digital file.

Lasers use mirrors to reflect monochromatic light and to cause coherence in the light waves.

  • Light is focused by a lens into a tight beam

  • Tight beam can be intensely hot and can travel for great distances.

  • Can be used to "read" the bumps and grooves of a CD-ROM to convert the information stored to a usable form.

  • Fiber-optic cables use laser light to transmit information.

  3. How do glasses improve the vision of the wearer?

    The lens of the eye refracts the light to focus it on the retina. For people who need glasses, the focal length of the lens changes and the light focuses on the wrong location, making the image blurry. Glasses correct the refraction so that it focuses on the retina again.
